Tips by Body Structure

— customised guidance

Endomorph (Prone to fat)

Focus on a modest calorie deficit, resistance training 3–5×/week, and monitor carbs. High protein intake (1.6–2.2g/kg) supports muscle retention.

  • Cardio for metabolic health
  • Strength + compound lifts
  • Reduce processed carbs

Ectomorph (Thin)

Hard gainer — aim for a calorie surplus with progressive overload training. Frequent meals and calorie-dense nutritious foods help (oats, nuts, dairy).

  • Higher carbs and calories
  • Focus on heavy compound lifts
  • 3–5 meals + snacks

Mesomorph (Athletic)

Responds well to training. Balance protein and carbs, periodise workouts between strength and conditioning.

  • Maintain protein intake
  • Mix strength & hypertrophy
  • Adjust calories per goal

Overweight / Obese

Sustainable calorie deficit, focus on activity increase, behaviour change. Seek medical help for major disease risk.

  • Low-impact cardio
  • Progressive resistance training
  • High protein, veg forward plates

Sarcopenic / Older Adult

Maintain muscle via resistance training and slightly higher protein per meal. Ensure vitamin D and calcium where needed.

General Healthy Living Tips

— habits that matter

Sleep

7–9 hours for most adults; sleep drives recovery and appetite regulation.

Hydration

Drink water across the day; tailor to activity and climate.

Progressive Overload

Increase load, reps or volume gradually to keep adapting.
